## Design Context

### Users
Readers who want to grasp a book's core structure quickly through a visually memorable page. The primary use case is shareable study content: a page that works both as personal review material and as a public-facing summary artifact.

### Brand Personality
Thoughtful, sharp, exploratory. The interface should feel intellectually serious, slightly provocative, and rewarding to inspect slowly rather than optimized for bland speed-reading.

### Aesthetic Direction
For philosophical book pages, favor concept-first visual systems instead of generic cards. Use motion and interaction to reveal relationships between ideas, tension, and transformation. Avoid startup-dashboard styling, neon sci-fi clichés, and decorative effects that do not deepen comprehension.

### Design Principles
1. Make abstract concepts spatial and dynamic so readers can feel the logic, not just read it.
2. Preserve literary seriousness while still delivering memorable visual moments.
3. Let interaction clarify asymmetry, uncertainty, and transformation rather than distract from them.
4. Use progressive enhancement so the static page remains clear even when advanced effects are unavailable.
5. Keep the visual language distinctive and conceptually tied to the book's thesis.
